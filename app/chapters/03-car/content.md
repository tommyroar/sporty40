Juneau has no road connections to the outside world — but it has about 50 miles of spectacular road to itself. Locals call driving north on Glacier Highway "going out the road," and having a car makes all of this ours.

**Mendenhall Glacier** — The only glacier in Southeast Alaska you can drive to. 13 miles from downtown, with trails to Nugget Falls and a close-up view of the ice face across the lake.

**Eagle Beach** — A wide black-sand beach at the edge of Lynn Canal, about 30 minutes north. Beachcombing, salmon fishing, and good whale-watching from shore.

**Point Bridget State Park** — A 7-mile round-trip hike out to a coastal point where sea lions and seals haul out in the surf. One of the best walks in the state.

**Shrine of St. Therese** — A small stone chapel on a causeway island, with views down Lynn Canal toward the Chilkat Mountains and frequent humpback whale sightings.

**Jensen-Olson Arboretum** — A quiet waterfront garden tucked along the highway, with mountain views across the inlet.
