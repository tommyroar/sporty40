The return trip trades sea lanes for asphalt — a two-day drive through some of the most remote and dramatic highway country in North America.

**Haines Ferry** — The journey begins with the [Alaska Marine Highway](https://dot.alaska.gov/amhs/) from Juneau north to Haines (4.5 hours). Haines sits at the head of Lynn Canal surrounded by glaciated peaks, and the ferry ride alone is worth the ticket.

**Haines Junction & Kluane** — From Haines the road climbs immediately into the Chilkat Range and crosses into the Yukon. Kluane National Park stretches along the Alaska Highway for miles — the largest non-polar icefield in the world lies just behind the front range, invisible from the road but present in the scale of everything.

**Whitehorse** — The Yukon capital is a good overnight stop, with a surprising dining scene and the [SS Klondike](https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/lhn-nhs/yt/ssklondike) sternwheeler drydocked on the riverbank as a national historic site.

**Stewart-Cassiar Highway** — Rather than backtracking on the Alaska Highway, the Cassiar (Hwy 37) cuts south through northwestern BC — wilder, less-traveled, with free-range bison in the road near Liard River and dramatic river canyon scenery all the way to the Skeena Valley.

**Back to Seattle** — The final leg crosses into Washington at Sumas and runs south through the Cascades foothills into the city. The mileage is roughly 1,800 miles door to door; the scenery keeps you honest the whole way.
