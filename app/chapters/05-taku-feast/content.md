A de Havilland Otter floatplane lifts off from the [downtown seaplane base](https://wingsairways.com/tours/taku-lodge-feast-5-glacier-seaplane-discovery/) and flies 30 miles over five glaciers — Norris, Taku, Hole-in-the-Wall, East Twin, and West Twin — to land at a 1923 lodge on the Taku River. Every seat is a window seat.

The lodge has been on the National Register of Historic Places for over a century and is reachable only by floatplane. You spend about two hours there: wild Alaska salmon grilled over alderwood, homemade baked beans, sourdough biscuits, and drinks chilled with actual glacier ice. Black bears are frequently spotted in the area.
