# Research

## Alaska Ferry

### Route & Duration
- **~62 hours / 3 days**, ~800 miles through the Inside Passage
- Stops: Bellingham → Ketchikan → Wrangell → Petersburg → Juneau (some sailings also hit Sitka)
- The **MV Columbia** runs the mainline weekly roundtrip Bellingham–Skagway; MV Kennicott is scheduled but likely won't operate due to crew availability

### Schedule
- 2026 summer schedule open for booking: **May 1 – Sept 30, 2026**
- Departures from Bellingham are **Fridays** (weekly roundtrip Bellingham ↔ Skagway)
- Columbia departs southbound from Ketchikan Wednesday afternoons, northbound Sunday mornings → implies ~Friday Bellingham departure
- Book at https://bookamhs.alaska.gov/book/sailing-search/journeySearch or call (800) 642-0066
- **Dynamic pricing** — book early, fares rise as availability drops
- Full timetable/calendar: https://dot.alaska.gov/amhs/schedules.shtml (interactive, not scrapeable)

### Accommodations (sold separately from passenger fare)
| Option | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Cabin** | Bunk-style berths, most with private bath. Charged per cabin (not per person). Book well in advance for Bellingham sailings. |
| **Solarium** | Covered upper deck with overhead heaters. Popular for sleeping in sleeping bags. |
| **Recliner lounge** | Indoor recliners; people roll out sleeping bags here too. |

### Practical notes
- Check in **2 hours early** at Bellingham
- Crossing **Queen Charlotte Sound** (open sea between BC and SE Alaska) can be rough — bring seasickness meds
- Bring quarters for lockers
- Crew are state employees and cannot accept tips
